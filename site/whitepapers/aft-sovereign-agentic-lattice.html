Problem Statement

Current agentic AI systems often operate as loosely coordinated model chains rather than rigorously governed, auditable, and reproducible collaboration systems. In high-consequence environments, agentic workflows need stronger foundations for communication, state tracking, simulation linkage, evidence preservation, and validation boundaries.

AFT Proposed Direction

Advanced Frontier Technologies is developing the SMILEQT Sovereign Lattice Runtime, integrated with Paladin Governance and the T.R.E.Y. OMEGA simulation layer. The architecture is designed as a prototype-backed, validation-first, 21-agent discrete lattice system for governed agent collaboration, simulation-driven principle extraction, and reproducible evidence packaging.

Core Components

  • SMILEQT Sovereign Lattice Runtime: Structured multi-agent coordination for role separation, state tracking, and traceable reasoning.
  • Paladin Governance: Oversight layer for priority handling, evidence controls, policy boundaries, and safety posture.
  • T.R.E.Y. OMEGA Simulation Layer: Physics-informed and lattice-oriented simulation workflows for experimentation and principle extraction.
  • BrainV3 Cause-Effect Reasoning: Causal reasoning support for hypothesis tracking and system-state interpretation.
  • Docker/MCP Deployment Skeleton: Modular local-first deployment model for reproducible service packaging and transition experiments.
